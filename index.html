Kshitiz (क्षितिज)

I am a first-year MSR student at the Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University, co-advised by Aviral Kumar and Zackory Erickson.

My research focuses on vision–language model (VLM) reasoning for robotics and real-world reinforcement learning (RWRL). I build manipulation systems that can understand instructions, ground language in perception, and plan robust actions in unstructured homes and labs. On the learning side, I study data-efficient, safe RWRL that lets robots autonomously improve over time—from large, messy, off-policy logs or continual on-robot data—while respecting safety constraints and minimizing human supervision.

Research (Focus)

Real-world perception-and-action systems operate amid shifting lighting, clutter, and goals. My work aims to make robot manipulation generalize and self-improve under these shifts.

  • VLM Reasoning for Manipulation: Grounding open-ended language commands into scene understanding, affordance discovery, and multi-step plans; integrating VLM priors with low-level control for robust long-horizon tasks.
  • Real-World RL for Autonomous Improvement: Data-efficient, safe learning on real robots using off-policy logs, constrained objectives, and continual updates; minimizing resets, annotations, and sim-to-real gaps.
  • Imitation + RL: Leveraging demonstrations and tele-op traces to bootstrap policies, then improving online with uncertainty-aware exploration and risk-sensitive control.

I care about methods that are deployable—easy to run on physical robots, resilient to distribution shift, and transparent about failure modes.
